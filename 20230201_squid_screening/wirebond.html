Wirebonding: 2 - 12 hr per board

  • Mount the PCB onto the wirebonding block
  • Wirebond the row selects, making sure to start at the copper PCB pads and end on the NbTi pads of the Squids. There are a total of 11 row selects per chip, with an input and output, for a total of 22 wirebonds per chip. A full board has a max of 24 chips, for a maximum of 528 bonds.
  • Wirebond the inputs in a stitch pattern on the Squid chip, ensuring the input coils are all connected in series. Ensure that the bonds are in the middle of the pad so that the bond surface does not touch two different pads. Keeping the bonds short and triangular shaped will also decrease the probability of cross-shorts. Then, wirebond the first and last input on the chip to the PCB. There are a total of 11 inputs, requiring 10 wirebonds to connect them in series. Adding the two wirebonds for connecting the inputs to the PCB, this is a total of 12 wirebonds per chip. This is a total of 288 max bonds per board.
  • Wirebond the feedback and output pads. These bonds on the sides of the chips are mostly straightforward except for the bonds on the outside of row X. These bonds are in a pentagonal shape with crossing wires, increasing the chance of contact shorts. Great care must be taken to ensure ideal bond lengths. There are a total of 8 bonds per chip, for a maximum of 192 bonds per board.
  • Once all the wirebonds are complete, either store the board in a nitrogen cabinet or move directly to room temperature screening. A single board has a maximum of 1008 bonds, from 42 bonds per chip.
